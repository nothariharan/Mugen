## Design Context

### Users
Non-technical compliance teams, HR managers, and risk officers. They need to understand the concept of bias and legal compliance thresholds without getting lost in mathematical complexity. They need clarity, confidence, and transparency to feel safe making auditing decisions.

### Brand Personality
Authoritative, Transparent, Humane, and Precise.
It should feel like a serious legal/auditing tool, not a generic SaaS or an AI playground.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: High-contrast, editorial layout that organizes complex data meticulously. We avoid the "AI look" (no cyan/purple gradients on dark backgrounds).
- **Theme**: Light mode (warm off-white/paper base) with deep, saturated accents (e.g., precise OKLCH deep blue or rich evergreen) to ensure maximum legibility in an office environment.
- **Typography**: 
  - Headlines: A powerful, highly legible geometric/grotesque sans (e.g., Bricolage Grotesque).
  - Body: A rigorous, clear typeface designed for data (e.g., Schibsted Grotesk) to convey precision.
- **Color**: Semantic OKLCH scale rooted in a primary deep, trustworthy hue.

### Design Principles
1. **Explain the Why**: Data visualizations must be accompanied by plain-English takeaway summaries. Never leave a number alone without context.
2. **Clear the Noise**: Use ample whitespace and distinct borders/dividers to prevent data overload. Separate distinct concepts aggressively.
3. **Pacing through Motion**: Use staggered reveals and smooth transitions (`tailwindcss-animate`) to guide the user's eye and establish the "story" of the audit, rather than slapping all data on the screen at once.
4. **Purposeful Color**: Colors strictly communicate state (Green=Pass, Red=Fail/Bias, Neutral=Info). Avoid decorative color splashes taking attention away from the data.
